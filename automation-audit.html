A practical starting point

Request an Automation Audit

Describe one process that consumes time, delays customers, or depends too heavily on memory. The goal is to identify realistic improvements—not to automate everything.

What the request covers

Give us enough context to examine the workflow

Your answers help separate a useful automation opportunity from a process that first needs clarification or a different kind of improvement.

CONTEXT

Current process

What starts the work, who owns each step, and which tools or documents are involved?

FRICTION

Current problem

Where do delays, rework, missed follow-up, duplicate entry, or inconsistent replies occur?

OUTCOME

Desired improvement

What should become faster, clearer, more consistent, or easier for the team to manage?

Audit request

Describe the workflow in plain language

You do not need a technical specification. Explain the task the way you would explain it to a new colleague.

  • Include the tools you currently use
  • Mention approximate frequency or volume
  • Explain where a person must make a judgment
  • Do not submit passwords, credentials, or sensitive records

Submitting this form is a request for a conversation, not an automatic service agreement or guarantee of suitability.

What happens next

A clear review before implementation

The response may recommend a focused automation, a smaller process change, additional discovery, or no automation at all if the workflow is not a good candidate.

Clarify

We may ask about volume, exceptions, system access, compliance, or the people responsible for the process.

Outline

A suitable opportunity can be described as a sequence of inputs, checks, decisions, actions, and fallback steps.

Decide

You can choose whether to proceed with guidance, a prototype, implementation, or further planning.
