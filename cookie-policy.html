Cookie Policy

This page explains the limited browser storage used by the website and how you can control it.

Last updated: July 23, 2026

What cookies and local storage are

Cookies are small text records that a website may ask a browser to store. Local storage is a similar browser feature that can retain a small preference on your device. These technologies can support essential functionality, remember preferences, measure usage, or support advertising depending on how a website is configured.

Related storage and access technologies can include pixels, scripts, software development kits, device identifiers, cache data, and browser fingerprinting. This policy uses “cookies” as a convenient collective term where the context permits, even when a technology does not create a traditional cookie file.

Common categories of storage

  • Strictly necessary: supports security, network delivery, requested features, or a user’s privacy choice.
  • Preferences: remembers optional display, language, or interface settings.
  • Analytics: measures visits, performance, and interaction patterns.
  • Advertising: supports ad delivery, attribution, profiling, or cross-site tracking.

A technology may be first-party, meaning it is set for this website, or third-party, meaning it is controlled by another service. Its duration may be limited to the current browser session or continue until a stated expiry date or manual deletion.

What this website currently uses

This website currently uses one local-storage preference to remember that you dismissed the cookie notice. The key is named flowready-cookie-notice-v1 by default and stores the value accepted. It does not identify you across other websites and is not used for advertising or analytics.

The contact forms also create ordinary in-page values needed to submit the form, such as the source page and form start time. Those values are sent with the form but are not stored as advertising or analytics cookies by the website.

Storage summary

Purpose: Remember the cookie-notice choice

Type: First-party local storage

Default key: flowready-cookie-notice-v1

Value: accepted

Duration: Until browser storage is cleared

Why this storage is used

The notice preference is used only to remember the choice you already made and avoid showing the same message on every page. It is treated as an essential preference rather than a tool for analytics, advertising, personalization, or cross-site identification.

Where applicable law requires consent for a future non-essential technology, that technology should remain disabled until a valid choice is obtained. Refusing a non-essential technology should not block access to ordinary website content unless the requested feature genuinely depends on it.

What is not currently enabled

No advertising, behavioral profiling, cross-site tracking, or website analytics cookies are currently enabled by the site’s own code. A hosting provider or security service may use strictly necessary technical measures to deliver and protect the website; its documentation should be reviewed when hosting is selected.

If optional analytics, embedded media, advertising, or other non-essential technologies are added later, this policy and the consent controls should be updated before those technologies are enabled where required.

Hosting and security providers

The infrastructure used to deliver the website may process technical requests, IP addresses, headers, or security signals and may use short-lived storage strictly to balance traffic, prevent abuse, or maintain a secure session. The exact technology depends on the provider selected for production hosting.

These essential infrastructure activities are different from advertising or behavioral analytics. Provider documentation should be reviewed at launch and whenever the hosting or security configuration changes.

How to control storage

You can clear this preference through your browser’s site-data or privacy settings. Clearing it will cause the notice to appear again. Blocking local storage may also cause the notice to reappear between visits but should not prevent you from reading the website.

Browser controls vary. Look for settings related to cookies, site data, local storage, privacy, or permissions. Clearing all site data may also remove preferences and signed-in sessions on other sites, depending on the option selected.

Private browsing and multiple devices

A private-browsing window may remove the preference when the window closes. Choices are generally specific to a browser profile and device, so dismissing the notice on one device may not carry over to another.

Do Not Track and opt-out signals

The site does not currently use behavioral advertising or cross-site analytics, so a Do Not Track or similar browser signal does not change its present behavior. If a legally recognized opt-out preference signal becomes relevant to future technology, it will be evaluated and honored where required.

Contact forms

Submitting a form sends information to the server and email recipient. This is separate from the browser-storage preference described above. See the Privacy Policy for information about form data.

Links and embedded content

Opening a third-party link takes you to a service that may use its own cookies or similar technology. If this website later embeds a video, map, scheduling tool, chat widget, social post, or payment feature, the provider may receive technical information when the feature loads.

Where required, non-essential embedded content should be delayed until the user makes an appropriate choice. The third party’s policy, rather than this policy, governs storage created after you leave this site or directly interact with its service.

How we review cookie use

We may periodically review the website code, consent interface, hosting configuration, and third-party integrations to check whether the storage summary remains accurate. A newly added script or provider should be assessed before publication and categorized by its actual purpose rather than its label.

Changes

This policy may be updated if website functionality, hosting, providers, or applicable requirements change. The date above will be revised when an updated version is published. If a material change requires a new consent choice, the stored preference may be reset so the updated options can be presented.
